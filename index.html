Computer Aided Design for Civil Engineers

Simple Living Abu Dhabi: Multi-Family Residential Building

Fall 2025 · Solo Project

Designed and documented a three-storey ACI 318-25 compliant reinforced concrete residential building with six apartment units. The workflow ran from 2D drafting in AutoCAD through a full 3D BIM model in Revit, covering the structural frame, pile-cap foundation system, staircases, railings, and landscaping. SAP2000 analysis under 1.2D+1.6L confirmed all member sizes and set reinforcement requirements. Revit Quantity Takeoff schedules were produced for concrete volumes, wall areas, and framing counts. Revit renderings were compared with AI-generated images to evaluate visualisation tools.

02

Civil Engineering Capstone — Structural Steel

The Almas Souq: Faceted Steel Market Hall

Fall 2025 · Team: K. Doucoure, D. Iric, J. Ritte

Developed the structural design for The Almas Souq, a market hall inspired by the diamond geometry of the Almas Marina community in Abu Dhabi. Starting from an AI-generated concept image, the team developed a replicated 2D truss portal-frame system (roof span 30 m, base 20 m, height approx. 13 m). Geometry was drafted in AutoCAD and imported into SAP2000. Wind loads were calculated per ASCE 7-16 for Exposure Category D (V = 44.5 m/s). A determinacy check found the 3D model requires 76 additional bracing members, with further refinement planned.

AutoCAD SAP2000 ASCE 7-16 Wind Load Design Steel Trusses 3D Modelling
03

Foundation Engineering Design — ENGR-UH 4431

Pile Group Foundation Design: Port Authority Building

Fall 2025 · Team: Aby Sall & Joan Ritte

Designed and compared two pile group configurations for a Port Authority building in Abu Dhabi using RSPile. Both used six 0.46 m diameter, 27.5 m elastic piles beneath a 10x5x1 m cap carrying 3,740 kN axial and 162 kN horizontal load. Configuration 1 (vertical 2x3 grid) gave 37 mm lateral displacement and axial loads ranging from 300 to 910 kN per pile. Configuration 2 (four battered piles and two vertical) cut lateral displacement to 3 mm and balanced loads to 500-750 kN, reducing peak bending moments from 60 kN.m to 13 kN.m. Hand calculations confirmed ultimate pile capacity of 5,492 kN with factors of safety of 6.0 to 7.3.

RSPile Geotechnical Analysis Pile Design Hand Calculations Technical Reporting
04

Steel Structures Design — ENGR-UH 3430

Pedestrian Walkway: Steel Truss and Connection Design

Fall 2025 · Course Project

Designed a two-level pedestrian walkway bridge spanning 4 x 15 ft with a 30 ft frame width, 12 ft truss depth and 20 ft column height. Full member design used double-angle shapes per AISC LRFD with ASCE 7-21 load combinations for dead load (6-in concrete floor slab, 4-in roof slab), floor live loads (90 psf), and 20 psf MEP roof allowance. Connection design at specified joints used bolted fasteners. Deliverables included full member design tables, detailed connection drawings, and a steel weight estimate.

AISC LRFD ASCE 7-21 Steel Connections Truss Analysis Technical Drawing

Computer Programming for Engineers — Fall 2022

Life Post-Stroke: Posture Monitoring Wearable Device

Fall 2022 · Team: J. Almasri, T. Gurudza, J. Ritte

Built an affordable posture-monitoring device for post-stroke rehabilitation patients using an M5Stack microcontroller with IMU (accelerometer and gyroscope). The C++ program computes spinal tilt angle and compares it against safe thoracic ranges. A real-time clock tracks bad posture duration before triggering vibration alerts, with shorter intervals for more severe angles. An inactivity timer prompts light exercise after one hour. Joan was responsible for the angle detection code and the bulk of the written report.
